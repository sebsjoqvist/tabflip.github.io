Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 30, 2026

TabFlip is a Safari extension that gives you Alt-Tab-style keyboard switching between browser tabs. This policy explains what data the extension touches and what it does with it.

Short version. TabFlip does not collect, transmit, or share any personal data. Everything it reads stays on your device, in Safari, for the lifetime of your session and your local extension storage. There is no server, no analytics, no third party.

What TabFlip accesses

To do its job, TabFlip uses three Safari extension permissions:

  • tabs — reads the title, URL, and favicon of every open tab in Safari so it can show them in the switcher list. This data is read on demand (when you open the switcher) and is only used to render the list inside the switcher overlay on your machine.
  • activeTab — temporarily grants TabFlip permission to inject the switcher overlay into the page you're currently looking at, but only at the moment you press the shortcut or click the toolbar icon. No permanent access to any website is granted.
  • storage — saves your preferences (theme, accent color, omni-search toggle) and the most-recently-used tab order to the extension's own local storage area, so they persist across browser restarts.

TabFlip never reads page content, form fields, passwords, or anything else from the websites you visit.

What TabFlip does NOT do

  • No data is sent over the network. The extension does not make any outbound HTTP requests.
  • No analytics, telemetry, crash reporting, or usage tracking.
  • No third-party SDKs, scripts, or services.
  • No cookies, fingerprinting, or cross-site identifiers.
  • No account, sign-in, or user identifier of any kind.
  • No advertising, profiling, or data sales.

Local data, in detail

All data TabFlip stores lives in the extension's local storage on your Mac. It is:

  • MRU stack — an array of tab IDs in most-recently-used order. Tab IDs are ephemeral Safari handles; they don't identify a site or page.
  • Settings — your chosen theme mode, accent color, and omni-search toggle.

You can clear this data at any time by removing TabFlip from Safari's extension list, or by quitting and reinstalling the host app.

Children's privacy

TabFlip does not knowingly collect data from anyone, of any age.

Changes to this policy

If material changes are ever made (e.g., adding a syncing feature), this page will be updated and the new effective date will be shown above. Material changes that affect data handling will be announced in the next release notes.
